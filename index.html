01 · The Deal

What's quoted on the term sheet

The brief sets three numbers. This valuation takes them at face value — the NPR 5 Cr team budget is modelled as real — and tests whether any plausible revenue path can carry that cost base.

Upfront franchise fee
NPR 1.5–2.0 Cr
One-time entry cost. Mid-point NPR 1.75 Cr, plus NPR 15 lakh setup capex and NPR 10 lakh opening working capital — NPR 2.0 Cr all-in.
Annual league fee
NPR 50 lakh/yr
Modelled at the quoted figure, escalated at CPI. A recurring drag on top of the team budget for all ten years.
Team maintenance budget
NPR 5 Cr/yr
Taken literally and allocated bottom-up across 12 cost lines. Against a generously-scaled NPR 2.2 Cr of Year-1 revenue, this is what sinks the deal.
!
The honesty move — state this up front
Observable NSL economics would support far leaner franchise opex than NPR 5 Cr. Rather than quietly override the brief, the model keeps the quoted budget and scales revenue up ~3.5× above the observable floor to stay internally consistent with a league whose teams can spend that much. Even under that deliberately generous treatment, the deal is deeply NPV-negative in every scenario — which strengthens the conclusion rather than weakening it.
02 · The Asset

Nepal's first franchise football league

Founded 2021, organised by NSEM with ANFA. Three seasons in, every match still plays at a single venue in Kathmandu — the one structural fact that shapes the entire revenue model.

"Centralised hosting means a franchise doesn't control matchday economics the way clubs in mature leagues do."
No home-and-away format; gate receipts accrue to the league, not the club. Revenue is dominated by a share of central sponsorship plus the franchise's own local deals — value creation lives in commercial growth, not the turnstile.
2025 season · 7 franchises
Champion prize money grew from NPR 3.5M (2021) to NPR 7–10M (2025) — a 20–26% CAGR that is the single best proxy for the league's commercial trajectory, and the anchor for the bull case.

06 · The Verdict

On the DCF, this is a clear decline at the quoted terms — value-destructive in every scenario, not a mispriced asset but a structurally loss-making one.

NPR −19.2 Cr
Probability-weighted NPV
Bear 25%Base 50%Bull 25%
No scenario survives — so the answer isn't a price, it's a no.

Bear, base and bull all destroy value: even the most optimistic case (broadcast deal in Year 3, fastest sponsorship growth, lowest WACC) lands at −NPR 11.9 Cr enterprise value. Weighted 25 / 50 / 25, expected NPV against the quoted entry is roughly −NPR 19 Cr. Picking the bull case alone to justify the cheque would be indefensible — there's zero evidence of an imminent broadcast deal. A disciplined no, with a clear price at which it becomes a yes, is the useful answer for a committee.

What changes it · A
Renegotiate the budget
Cut the annual team budget to ~NPR 1.7 Cr (≈35% of the quoted NPR 5 Cr). At base wage inflation, that is where NPV reaches break-even — the number to take into negotiation.
What changes it · B
Win a bigger revenue share
Secure a franchise agreement granting a materially larger slice of central and gate revenue. The NSEM/franchise split is the single largest structural unknown.
What changes it · C
Price the optionality
Justify it on strategic grounds outside the DCF — first-mover brand positioning or a marketing platform for the group, the way Ncell already uses NSL sponsorship.
DECLINE
AT QUOTED TERMSRenegotiate first
Do not acquire at NPR 1.5–2.0 Cr with a NPR 5 Cr team budget. This is not a cash business and the brief's cost base makes it value-destructive under every assumption — proceed only if the team budget is renegotiated to roughly a third, or the deal is underwritten on strategic value the DCF doesn't capture.
